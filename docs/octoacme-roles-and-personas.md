# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive user-centered design across the product lifecycle. They translate requirements into intuitive interfaces and validate that the product meets real user needs through research and usability testing.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Conduct usability research and testing sessions
- Define and maintain design standards and component libraries
- Hand off finalized designs to developers with clear specifications
- Participate in sprint planning and design reviews

### Goals
- Ensure the product is intuitive and accessible for target users
- Reduce usability issues discovered post-release
- Maintain design consistency across features and platforms

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- Prototype walkthroughs and usability test readouts
- Design handoff documentation and annotation in design tools

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts define measurement strategies, analyze project and product data, and provide actionable insights that support prioritization and continuous improvement decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Define key metrics and measurement plans in collaboration with Product Managers and Project Managers
- Build and maintain dashboards and reports for project and product health
- Analyze usage and performance data to surface trends and anomalies
- Provide data-driven insights for retrospectives and planning sessions
- Validate that feature launches meet success criteria

### Goals
- Ensure decisions are grounded in reliable, well-understood data
- Reduce time to insight by maintaining clear, accessible dashboards
- Identify and flag risks or regressions early through proactive monitoring

### Typical Communication
- Regular data readouts during planning and retrospectives
- Dashboard links and summary reports shared with the team
- Ad-hoc analysis requests from Product Managers and Project Managers

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, automation, and pipelines that enable reliable, repeatable delivery. They bridge development and operations to ensure systems are stable and deployable at any time.

### Responsibilities
- Design, maintain, and improve CI/CD pipelines and build automation
- Automate deployment, rollback, and environment provisioning processes
- Monitor system health and respond to incidents alongside the delivery team
- Champion infrastructure best practices, security hardening, and cost efficiency
- Collaborate with Developers on build, release, and observability requirements

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and repeatable software releases
- Minimize production incidents and mean time to recovery
- Maintain clear visibility into infrastructure health and deployment status

### Typical Communication
- Deployment status updates and incident reports shared with Project Managers and Developers
- Infrastructure documentation and runbooks
- On-call and incident response coordination channels

---

## Security Champion

### Role Summary
Security Champions advocate for security best practices throughout the design, development, and deployment lifecycle. They help the team identify and mitigate risks before they become vulnerabilities.

### Responsibilities
- Promote secure coding standards and review code or designs for security concerns
- Conduct or coordinate threat modeling and risk assessments at key project milestones
- Liaise with dedicated security teams and escalate findings when necessary
- Lead post-incident security reviews and drive remediation follow-through
- Ensure security requirements are captured in acceptance criteria and definition of done

### Goals
- Reduce the number and severity of security vulnerabilities reaching production
- Build a security-aware culture across the delivery team
- Ensure compliance with relevant security standards and policies

### Typical Communication
- Security review findings shared with Developers and Product/Project Managers
- Risk assessment summaries during planning and milestone reviews
- Incident debrief reports and remediation tracking

---

## Customer Success/Support Representative

### Role Summary
Customer Success/Support Representatives serve as the voice of the customer within the delivery team. They surface real-world feedback and support issues to inform priorities and improve the user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize user feedback from support channels, tickets, and customer conversations
- Communicate recurring pain points and feature requests to Product Managers
- Participate in post-release reviews to share customer impact observations
- Assist in creating or reviewing end-user documentation and release notes
- Help triage and reproduce reported issues in collaboration with Developers

### Goals
- Ensure the team understands how customers experience the product in practice
- Reduce the volume and impact of support escalations through proactive improvements
- Build positive customer relationships by closing the feedback loop effectively

### Typical Communication
- Support trend summaries and customer feedback reports shared with Product and Project Managers
- Participation in sprint reviews and post-release retrospectives
- Collaboration on help documentation and user-facing communications
